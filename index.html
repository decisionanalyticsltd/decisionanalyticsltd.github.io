Dundrum South Dublin A.C.

Analytical strategy development

Decision Analytics improved strategic decision-making via survey data collection and visualisation.

The background: Dundrum South Dublin Athletics Club (DSD) is an 800+ member sports club with significant influence in the athletic and sports community including competitive athletes and widely attended events such as the VHI women’s mini marathon.

The Challenge: DSD invested in a state-of-the-art athletics facility and has seen significant growth in recent years. This drove the need to define the future direction of the Club with a strategic plan for 2023-2025.

Our approach: Decision Analytics was asked to conduct a strategic review to ensure that DSD had a clear trajectory for years to come. The strategic review included board level workshops, a full members survey, focus groups and interviews. The output was a strategic plan for 2023-2025 including a roadmap, a set of priorities, and initiatives that DSD will use as a guiding light for quarterly planning.

We are excited to release the Strategic Plan in the coming months, stay tuned for an update.

Guiness Enterprise Centre

Resident needs analysis

Decision Analytics unlocks value-adding opportunities with insight from machine learning.

The Guinness Enterprise Centre is a startup incubator and co-working space. A recent investment of €11m expanded the facilities and resources on offer.

The Challenge: Decision Analytics was brought in to understand how to identify the needs of residents in a remote working world, how GEC can develop relationships between startups, and how GEC can connect startups with investors.

The Solution: Decision Analytics applied advanced analytics and data visualisation to the GEC CRM to make the community network more accessible. We established a quarterly data capture to track the developing status and needs of residents. Lastly, we used machine learning to highlight opportunities to connect startups where one startup has overcome a similar need in a similar sector.

The Output: The result of the project is a suite of tools that GEC can use to understand the needs of their residents and act to support startups and showcase the talent on offer in the incubator.

Online retailer

Web analytics platform

Our client, an online retailer specializing in the tourism sector, faced challenges in managing and analyzing their scattered data across various online channels and marketing campaigns. Seeking to streamline their operations and improve decision-making, they enlisted our help.

The Challenge: Challenges included data fragmentation with data scattered across multiple platforms, making analysis difficult. There was no centralized reporting and an absence of a unified reporting system hindered insights. There was limited visibility as inadequate tools led to a lack of visibility into customer behaviour and campaign performance.

The Solution: We worked closed with the client to design an ideal future state reporting solution. We consolidated many data sources into a single warehouse. We utilised Google Looker and GA4 analytics where we tailored solutions to fit the client's needs. Lastly we created intuitive dashboards for real-time insights.

The Output: The client now had a centralized data hub with simplified access to data. Enhanced reporting provided actionable insights for informed decisions. We built a framework for future expansion.

Software that improves your decision making.

Plug in to a shared community of EV charge points! Locate charging points on the E-Shared map, or create a listing and start earning passive income from your own charging point today.

We have built a fully operational prototype to test the market with this business concept and would love feedback from EV drivers - drop us a mail to get in touch.

The challenge: While the transition to Electric Vehicles has accelerated, the number of charging stations has not kept up with demand. There are now 80,000 EVs on Irish roads with roughly 2,000 public chargers falling short of the estimated 4,700 needed. With a target of a million EVs on the road in the next decade, Ireland faces a EV charging point supply challenge. According to the Society of the Irish Motor Industry (SIMI), to meet demand over the coming eight years, we need to install 100,000 fast-charging points.

The solution: We estimate that 10,000 existing home charge points can be used to reduce that shortfall. To address this opportunity, we have set up a web platform called E-shared, allowing EV owners to list their EV charge point as well as rent from others.

Our approach: As with all new business ideas, the first step was to research the market and understand the dynamics. We used data and analytics to model the projected trajectory of EV ownership and charge point demand. We researched global competitor strategy and narrowed in on a minimal viable product to satisfy customer expectations. We built a minimal viable product using Sharetribe and within a week from ideation to MVP we launched the concept.
